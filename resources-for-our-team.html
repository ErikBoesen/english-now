Online Curriculum Resources (watch this space, this list will grow)

  1. Bookshare, an ebook library that makes reading easier. Members can access a huge collection of ebooks and read their way with the most customizable ebooks for people with reading barriers. URL:  https://www.bookshare.org/home Login: info@english-now.com PW: Englishnow1 (Thanks, Jeannie, for this recommendation!)
  2. Breaking News English: Current events stories - includes mini-lessons with answers and audio available in five speeds.
  3. Englishpage.com: Grammar review and exercises on many topics, great for students to reference independently or use in class
  4. Readworks: a free resource for K-12 educators with readings searchable by grade/Lexile level/topic; used in MCPS
  5. Voice of America: many short news stories, kept current, with the ability to change the pace of playback sped
  6. National Public Radio: all your favorite programs, and many stories have transcripts that students can review as prework or together
  7. TED: TED Talks are a great resource generally, highlighted in National Geographic curriculum including Keynote, and available online with transcripts in English and often dozens of other languages--assigning TED Talks as prework then playing a segment (or reviewing a segment of the transcript, if audio is an issue) can be a great way to spark conversation.
